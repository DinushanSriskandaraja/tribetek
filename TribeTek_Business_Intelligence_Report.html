Key Finding: The website is visually professional and messaging is well-structured. The fatal issue is that the hero section displays "0+" for every impact metric (projects delivered, hours saved, years of experience). This is almost certainly a JS hydration bug — the counters animate from 0 but the real numbers are never populated. To any first-time visitor, TribeTek looks like it has zero experience. This is the single most damaging issue on the site right now.

The newsletter subscription box in the footer has no visible mechanism and no proof of audience — it needs to either be powered up with a real email tool (Mailchimp, ConvertKit) or removed.

  • Zero Social Media ActivityAll three channels appear dormant based on zero discoverable engagement data. A service business with no social proof is invisible to modern buyers who research before contacting anyone.
  • No Case Studies or Client TestimonialsThe entire website has no named clients, no quotes, no before/after stories. In a trust-driven B2B service sale, this is the single biggest conversion killer.
  • Blog Publishing Has StoppedLast article was September 2025 — 8 months of silence. Google penalises stale sites. Competitors who publish consistently will overtake TribeTek's SEO position.
🟡 Moderate Gaps — Address in 1–3 Months
  • LinkedIn is UnderinvestedTribeTek's ICP (business owners, ops managers) lives on LinkedIn, not Instagram. The current social strategy is targeting the wrong platform for B2B lead generation.
  • No Lead Magnet or Email CaptureThe newsletter CTA collects emails but there's no visible confirmation it works, no incentive to subscribe, and no email sequence built. This is a dead end in the funnel.
  • No Booking / Calendar ToolThe "Request a Free Consultation" CTA goes to a contact form — a high-friction path. Adding a Calendly or Cal.com link would increase conversion rate significantly.
  • No SEO Keyword Strategy VisibleBlog titles like "Top Tech Stacks for Scalable Applications" have weak search intent alignment. No automation + Sri Lanka specific targeting is happening.
  • No Review Platform PresenceTribeTek is not listed on Clutch, GoodFirms, or TechBehemoths — the directories buyers use to discover and vet software agencies in Sri Lanka.

  • LinkedIn Thought Leadership AngleThe founder(s) sharing real automation case stories, "before/after workflows", or "how we automated X for a client" posts would generate massive B2B trust. No competitor in LK is doing this well.
